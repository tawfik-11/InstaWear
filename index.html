02 — Abstract

In a Nutshell

InstaWear is an interactive virtual fitting room system designed to revolutionize the retail apparel experience. The system integrates a Microsoft Kinect v2 depth camera with a custom digital gauge tool to capture full-body measurements with exceptional precision — achieving circumferential measurement accuracy up to >95%.

Using the captured data, the system generates a personalized SMPL-X parametric avatar that faithfully mirrors the user's body dimensions. Facial geometry captured from the RGB feed is overlaid onto the avatar, creating a truly immersive, photo-realistic experience. Users can then browse a digital wardrobe, try on garments virtually, and receive instant fit feedback — whether a size runs tight, loose, or true-to-fit — along with smart size recommendations whenever a better option exists.

The project directly addresses a critical gap in modern retail: the inability to objectively verify clothing fit before purchase, which remains the leading driver of return rates globally. InstaWear bridges this gap by merging computer vision, 3D body modeling, and augmented reality into a seamless kiosk-based solution.

04 — Project Scope

What We Cover

InstaWear targets the in-store fitting room experience, helping retail brands modernize their customer journey. The scope encompasses:

  • 3D body scanning via Kinect v2 depth sensing and IR skeleton tracking.
  • Circumferential measurement of chest, waist, and hips using a marker-embedded digital gauge with real-time positional validation.
  • SMPL-X avatar generation personalized to the user's exact body shape and facial features.
  • Virtual garment try-on with real-time draping simulation and fit classification.
  • Size recommendation engine suggesting optimal sizing based on captured measurements.

The system is designed for deployment as a kiosk-based installation inside physical retail environments — no wearables or special gear required.

05 — Market Problem

The Pain We Solve

The global fashion industry loses billions annually due to sizing mismatch. Shoppers either avoid online purchases out of uncertainty, or return items post-delivery — both outcomes hurting retailers and frustrating customers.

Even in physical stores, traditional fitting rooms are time-consuming and provide no objective size guidance. Customers rely on subjective feel and inconsistent brand sizing charts, leading to poor purchase confidence and high churn.

InstaWear replaces guesswork with data-driven precision — giving every shopper a personalized, immersive fitting experience backed by computer vision and 3D body modeling.

06 — Introduction

How It Works

The InstaWear experience begins the moment a user steps in front of the Kinect v2 camera. The sensor combines a 1080p RGB camera with a depth sensor and IR skeleton tracker, capturing the user's full 3D body silhouette in real time.

To enhance circumferential measurement precision — where depth scanning alone is insufficient — the user interacts with a digital gauge device equipped with optical markers. The system's computer vision pipeline reads the marker position relative to the body, confirming whether the tape sits correctly at the chest, waist, and hip landmarks. On-screen prompts guide the user until each measurement is validated.

These measurements feed into the SMPL-X parametric body model, generating a fully rigged, anatomically accurate avatar. Facial geometry from the RGB feed completes the likeness, making the experience genuinely immersive.

The avatar is then dressed with garments from the store's digital catalog. A physics-based draping engine simulates how each piece falls on the user's specific body, and a fit analysis module classifies every garment as Tight, Comfortable, or Loose, suggesting the correct size variant when needed.
